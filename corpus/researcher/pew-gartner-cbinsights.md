# Pew Research Center + Gartner + CB Insights

## Why they matter to the Researcher
These three organizations are the reference standard for secondary research that the Researcher agent cites in competitive matrices, market sizing, and trend backgrounders. Each occupies a distinct lane and should not be used interchangeably: **Pew** for rigorous public-opinion and demographic data with transparent methodology, **Gartner** for enterprise-software vendor landscape and adoption curves, **CB Insights** for private-company, startup, and emerging-tech market mapping. Treating them as a tiered toolkit — rather than defaulting to whichever is most convenient — is what separates a credible competitive brief from a Googled one. It also lets the Researcher present options with proper provenance, so the PM-lite can weigh claims against the reliability of their source. The Researcher's job is to cite, annotate, and reconcile — not to launder secondary data as first-party insight.

## Signature works & primary sources
- Pew U.S. Survey Methodology — https://www.pewresearch.org/u-s-survey-methodology/ — How ATP is sampled, recruited, and weighted.
- Pew American Trends Panel — https://www.pewresearch.org/the-american-trends-panel/ — Probability-based online panel; the backbone of most Pew U.S. work.
- Gartner Magic Quadrant methodology — https://www.gartner.com/en/research/methodologies/magic-quadrants-research — Vendor evaluation on Ability to Execute × Completeness of Vision.
- Gartner Hype Cycle — https://www.gartner.com/en/research/methodologies/gartner-hype-cycle — Technology maturity arc.
- CB Insights Research — https://www.cbinsights.com/research/ — Market maps, tech trend reports, funding data.
- CB Insights Market Map Maker — https://www.cbinsights.com/what-we-offer/platform/market-map-maker/ — Tool for categorizing and visualizing private-company landscapes.

## Core principles (recurring patterns)
- **Pew: probability sampling + transparent weighting.** Pew's credibility rests on probability-based recruitment (ATP now via address-based sampling from the USPS Delivery Sequence File) and public methodology documentation. Every survey ships with a methods appendix.
- **Gartner: two-axis vendor scoring.** Magic Quadrant = Ability to Execute (financial viability, market responsiveness, product, sales, customer base) vs. Completeness of Vision (innovation, market understanding). Four quadrants: Leaders, Challengers, Visionaries, Niche Players. Informed by 500k+ client interactions and 27k+ vendor briefings.
- **CB Insights: private-market mapping.** Tracks private-company signals (funding, hiring, patents, partnerships) to generate market maps and emergent-category identification. Strong for pre-IPO and startup landscapes where Gartner has thin coverage.
- **Always name the source tier.** Primary (your own interviews) > first-party provider methodology (Pew) > analyst synthesis (Gartner) > aggregated signals (CB Insights). Don't mix tiers silently.
- **Methodology disclosures are not optional.** If a source doesn't publish methodology, downgrade the claim.

## Concrete templates, checklists, or artifacts the agent can reuse
- **Source-tier table for every competitive / market brief:**
  | Claim | Source | Source type | Methodology link | Date | Confidence |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|
- **Competitive matrix column set (hybrid of all three):**
  - Vendor name
  - Segment / category (from CB Insights market map)
  - Ability to execute proxy: funding stage, revenue if public, customer count
  - Vision proxy: stated roadmap themes, public R&D signals
  - Last analyst mention (Gartner MQ / Forrester Wave / CB Insights report + date)
  - Primary use case
  - Pricing model
  - Notable customers
  - Gaps / risks
  - Source URLs
- **Pew citation skeleton:** "Pew Research Center, [study title], [date]. Based on ATP Wave [N], N=[sample], margin of error ±[X]%. Methodology: [url]."
- **Gartner MQ reading checklist:** who's in each quadrant, who moved since last report, who was dropped, who's missing entirely (often the interesting signal).
- **CB Insights market map reuse pattern:** pull the category tree → annotate each company with primary-research notes → strip CB Insights branding before reuse; always link back.

## Where they disagree with others
- Gartner's quadrant-style snapshots vs. Torres's continuous discovery: Gartner is a periodic, secondary-data synthesis of vendor positioning; Torres is a continuous, first-party customer-needs framework. They answer different questions; don't substitute one for the other.
- Pew's probability-sample rigor vs. CB Insights' signals-based inference: Pew requires declared probability sampling and published weighting; CB Insights uses behavioral and financial signals that are strong but not survey-grade. Pew claims get cited at face value; CB Insights claims should be framed as directional.
- Gartner's analyst-synthesis model vs. bottom-up community evaluations (ThoughtWorks Radar, Stack Overflow survey): Gartner is top-down expert judgment weighted toward enterprise buyers; the Radar is practitioner consensus from one firm's engagements. Use both, cite the difference.
