# Teresa Torres

## Why they matter to the Researcher
Teresa Torres is the modern standard for turning raw customer research into structured, actionable product inputs. Her opportunity-solution tree is the bridge between interview notes (the Researcher's raw output) and a product-decision surface the PM-lite can reason over — without the Researcher crossing the line into making product decisions. Torres's framework lets the Researcher present a shaped, traceable map of unmet needs with multiple candidate solutions and test plans, so tradeoffs are explicit and decision rights stay with the product role. For Notion research pages, her structure provides a consistent schema (outcome → opportunity → solution → assumption test) that reads the same every time and keeps insights tied back to actual interview quotes.

## Core principles (recurring patterns)
- **Outcomes over outputs.** Root the tree in a product outcome (a measurable customer behavior or sentiment), never a business metric or a feature. This is what keeps the Researcher from smuggling in product decisions.
- **Story-based interviews only.** Capture specific past behaviors ("tell me about the last time you…"), never hypotheticals or stated preferences. Minimum three to four story-based interviews before you start mapping.
- **Opportunities are discovered, not invented.** If an opportunity didn't come from an interview, it doesn't go on the tree. "When we generate opportunities off the top of our heads, we bring our own biases."
- **Multiple solutions per opportunity, always.** Single-solution branches are a smell — they hide that you stopped exploring. Force at least three candidate solutions so tradeoffs are visible.
- **Assumption tests, not feature bets.** Each solution leaf decomposes into the riskiest assumptions (desirability, viability, feasibility, usability) and the cheapest test that would falsify them.
- **Continuous, not episodic.** Discovery cadence is weekly touchpoints, not quarterly research sprints.

## Concrete templates, checklists, or artifacts the agent can reuse
- **Opportunity-solution tree skeleton (Notion toggle layout):**
  - Outcome (H1) — measurable customer behavior, current baseline, target
  - Opportunity clusters (H2) — each with 2–5 child opportunities
  - Opportunities (H3) — verbatim customer pain/need + source interview ID + frequency
  - Solutions (bulleted under opportunity) — 3+ candidates, one-line description each
  - Assumption tests (sub-bullets) — riskiest assumption, test method, success criteria
- **Story-based interview script skeleton:**
  1. Warm-up: role, context, last time they did [activity]
  2. Story prompt: "Walk me through the most recent time you…"
  3. Probe on concrete artifacts ("what did you open next?")
  4. Probe on emotional peaks ("what was the most frustrating moment?")
  5. Probe on workarounds ("what did you try before that?")
  6. No feature pitches, no hypothetical questions
- **Interview snapshot (one-pager per interview):** participant context, verbatim quotes, observed behaviors, emerging opportunities, open questions. Pasted into Notion and linked from every tree node it feeds.
- **Weekly discovery checklist:** 3+ interviews completed, tree updated, at least one assumption test designed, one test shipped to a customer.

## Where they disagree with others
- Torres vs. Gartner / CB Insights quadrant-style snapshots: Torres's tree is continuously updated and grounded in first-party interviews; quadrant reports are periodic syntheses of secondary data. Use Torres for product discovery, not for market sizing.
- Torres vs. NN/g's method-selection framing: NN/g treats method choice as the central discipline; Torres treats story-based interviews as the default and focuses her rigor on synthesis structure instead.
- Torres vs. "big research study" traditions: she explicitly opposes infrequent, large-sample studies in favor of small, weekly, continuous touchpoints.
