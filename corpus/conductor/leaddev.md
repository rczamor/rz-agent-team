# LeadDev

## Why they matter to the Conductor
LeadDev is the working aggregator of the rest of this corpus — Fournier, Larson, Orosz, Reilly all give LeadDev talks and publish there. For the Conductor, LeadDev serves two concrete functions: (1) it's the canonical source for "what are top engineering leaders actually talking about this quarter," which keeps the Conductor's review standards current rather than frozen in 2020; and (2) its research reports (Engineering Team Performance Report, StaffPlus Report) provide benchmarks the Conductor can cite when arbitrating "is this normal?" questions — PR throughput, review-latency expectations, incident frequencies, staff-engineer scope. It's also the best source for case studies of specific coordination problems (Linear adoption, async-first teams, AI rollout) the Conductor will encounter.

## Signature works & primary sources
- LeadDev.com — https://leaddev.com — Articles, video talks, research reports.
- Engineering Team Performance Report (annual) — https://leaddev.com/reports — Benchmark data on team structure, cadences, tooling.
- StaffPlus track — https://leaddev.com/staffplus — Content specifically for staff/principal engineers.
- Conferences: LDX3 London, LeadDev New York, LeadDev Berlin — annual flagship events with talk recordings published free.
- Newsletters: the weekly digest and role-specific newsletters (Engineering Manager, StaffPlus, DirectorPlus).

## Core principles (recurring patterns)
1. **Engineering leadership is a distinct discipline, not "management of technical people."** The skills are specific and teachable.
2. **Cross-role vocabulary matters.** Tech Lead, EM, Staff, Principal, Director — LeadDev normalizes these so conversations across companies are coherent.
3. **Case studies > abstract advice.** Most valuable talks describe one team's specific problem and resolution, not "10 tips for…"
4. **Research beats vibes.** The annual reports replace anecdata with representative benchmarks.
5. **AI adoption is the dominant 2025-2026 topic.** Code review, developer productivity metrics, and team composition are all being re-examined under AI.
6. **Audience-segmented content.** Engineering Manager, StaffPlus, DirectorPlus tracks acknowledge that advice doesn't transfer uniformly up the ladder.

## Concrete templates, checklists, or artifacts the agent can reuse

**"What's normal?" benchmark lookup (when arbitrating scope/cadence disputes):**
When an agent claims "this is too many PRs per week" or "this review cycle is too slow," consult the most recent Engineering Team Performance Report for the industry median + top-quartile numbers. Cite the report version in the Linear comment.

**Talk-synthesis template (when reading a LeadDev talk for team use):**
1. What specific problem did this team face?
2. What constraints made the standard answer wrong?
3. What did they try? What failed first?
4. What's the generalizable takeaway vs. what's context-specific to them?
5. What would we need to change to apply this?

**StaffPlus scope-calibration check:** Before promoting work to "staff-level," verify it matches LeadDev StaffPlus-track content patterns — cross-team impact, org-shaping artifacts, not just senior-IC technical depth. Prevents title inflation.

**Conference talk evaluation filter (avoiding cargo-culting):**
- Is this speaker from a company with similar constraints (size, stage, product)?
- Did they describe what *didn't* work?
- Are the specifics portable, or was success dependent on their unique context (founder buy-in, huge budget, specific hire)?
- Is there a counter-example on the same LeadDev site?

**Hot-topic tracker (quarterly, for the Conductor):**
Browse LeadDev front page once a quarter. Note the three topics appearing most. If any aren't reflected in the Conductor's working process, evaluate whether to update.

## Where they disagree with others
- **LeadDev vs. individual voices:** LeadDev tends to aggregate and normalize; the individual experts (Larson, Fournier) take sharper positions. Use LeadDev for "what's the range?", individuals for "what's the right call?"
- **LeadDev vs. StaffEng:** LeadDev covers the entire leadership ladder including management; StaffEng is strictly the IC staff+ track. They partially overlap at the StaffPlus track but differ in audience breadth.
- **LeadDev vs. Pragmatic Engineer:** Both cover practice, but Orosz writes with a sharper POV and deeper single-company reporting. LeadDev is broader and more conference-event-driven.
